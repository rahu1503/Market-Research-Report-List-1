<p><p>Grass jelly, also known as leaf jelly or xiancao, is a popular dessert in various Asian countries, especially in China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, and Southeast Asia. It is made from the leaves of Mesona chinensis, a plant native to these regions. The leaves are boiled and the resulting liquid is mixed with starch and sweeteners to create a jelly-like consistency.</p><p>Grass jelly has a unique texture with a slightly bitter taste which is often balanced with the addition of sweeteners such as syrup or honey. It is typically served cold and can be eaten on its own or added to other dessert dishes like shaved ice or bubble tea, making it a versatile ingredient.</p><p>The grass jelly market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years.
