<p><p>Motorcycle Anti-lock Braking Systems (ABS) are designed to prevent the wheels from locking up and skidding during braking. There are two types of ABS systems in the market. Single channel ABS utilizes a single wheel sensor to monitor and control the front or rear brake, allowing the other wheel to lock up. Multi-channel ABS uses multiple wheel sensors, each monitoring and controlling the braking of individual wheels independently. This provides more precise control and stability during braking, reducing the risk of accidents caused by wheel lock-up.</p></p>

After loading, on the other hand, involves retrofitting ABS onto existing motorcycles by consumers or authorized service centers. Both front loading and after loading aim to enhance rider safety by preventing wheels from locking during braking, thus maintaining better control and reducing the risk of accidents.</p></p>
