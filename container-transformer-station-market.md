Rated Medium Voltage container stations can operate at a slightly higher voltage level, usually between 1000 and 35,000 volts. On the other hand, Rated High Voltage container stations are designed to handle high voltage levels, often exceeding 35,000 volts. These distinctions in voltage ratings allow the container transformer stations to cater to different power distribution requirements.</p></p>
