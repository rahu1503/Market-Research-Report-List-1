<p><p>The steel market is divided into two main types: carbon steel and alloy steel. Carbon steel is made from iron and carbon, with no other alloying elements. It is the most commonly used steel type due to its affordability and wide availability. On the other hand, alloy steel is made by mixing carbon steel with other elements such as chromium, nickel, or manganese. This enhances its properties, making it more resistant to corrosion, stronger, and able to withstand higher temperatures. Alloy steel is often used in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and construction.</p></p>
