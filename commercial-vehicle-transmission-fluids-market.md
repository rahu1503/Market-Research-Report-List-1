<p><p>Commercial vehicle transmission fluids are lubricants specifically designed for use in transmissions of commercial vehicles such as trucks, buses, and off-road vehicles. These fluids provide various functions, including cooling, friction reduction, cleaning, and protecting against wear and corrosion.</p><p>The global commercial vehicle transmission fluids market has been growing steadily in recent years and is expected to continue its growth trajectory. The market's growth can be attributed to several factors, including the increasing demand for commercial vehicles across the globe, the growing preference for automatic transmissions in commercial vehicles, and the need for extended service intervals and reduced maintenance costs.</p><p>An important trend in the commercial vehicle transmission fluids market is the shift towards synthetic fluids. Synthetic fluids offer superior performance and durability compared to conventional mineral-based fluids. They have better temperature resistance, oxidation stability, and lubricity, making them suitable for commercial vehicles that operate in extreme conditions. Additionally, the growing trend of electrification in commercial vehicles is expected to create new opportunities for transmission fluid suppliers, as electric commercial vehicles still require transmission fluids for their drivetrain components.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing significant investments in research and development activities by key players to introduce advanced transmission fluids with improved fuel efficiency and reduced environmental impact.
