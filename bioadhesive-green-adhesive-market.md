<p><p>The bioadhesive market primarily consists of two types: plant-based and animal-based adhesives. Plant-based adhesives are derived from renewable resources such as corn, soybeans, or starch, making them environmentally friendly. They offer high adhesion and are often used in industries like packaging and paper. On the other hand, animal-based adhesives are derived from natural sources like animal hides or bones. They are known for their strong adhesive properties and find applications in industries like woodworking and construction. Both plant-based and animal-based adhesives serve as sustainable alternatives to conventional petroleum-based adhesives.</p></p>
