<p><p>The Nuclear Molecular Imaging System market includes various types of imaging systems such as Positron Emission Tomography (PET), Single-photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT), and Planar Scintigraphy Systems. PET uses a radioactive tracer to measure metabolic activity and detect diseases. SPECT uses a radioactive substance to create 3D images of organs, helping diagnose conditions like heart diseases. Planar Scintigraphy Systems capture 2D images to detect bone, thyroid, or lung abnormalities. These imaging systems play a crucial role in diagnosing and monitoring diseases by providing detailed information about the body's internal processes.</p></p>
