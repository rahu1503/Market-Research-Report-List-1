<p><p>Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplantation (HSCT) involves the transfer of hematopoietic stem cells from a donor to a recipient, in order to restore normal blood cell production. Autologous transplants use stem cells sourced from the patient themselves, while allogenic transplants use stem cells sourced from a genetically matched donor. Autologous transplants are typically used in diseases where the patient's own stem cells are healthy, while allogenic transplants are used when the recipient's own stem cells are damaged or inadequate. These different types of transplants cater to specific medical needs and have their own market segments.</p></p>
