<p><p>Electroactive polymers (EAP) are materials that can change shape or size when subjected to an electric field. There are two main types of EAP: electronic type and ionic type. Electronic type EAP responds to electrical stimuli through the movement of electrons within the polymer, while ionic type EAP uses mobile ions to generate movement. Both types have diverse applications, such as actuators, sensors, artificial muscles, and energy harvesting devices. The electronic type is often used in electronic devices, and the ionic type finds applications in biomimetic systems and soft robotics.</p></p>
