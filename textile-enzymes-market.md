<p><p>Textile enzymes are specialized bio-catalysts used in the textile industry to enhance various processes. Pectinase enzymes facilitate the removal of pectin, a substance present in plant tissues, from fabrics. Cellulase enzymes break down cellulose, a major component of cotton, to improve fabric softness and reduce dyeing time. Amylase enzymes are utilized for starch removal from textiles. Laccase enzymes enhance dye fixation and color fastness. Catalase enzymes aid in the removal of hydrogen peroxide, commonly used in bleaching. Other types of textile enzymes include proteases, lipases, and oxidases, each serving specific purposes in textile production.</p></p>
