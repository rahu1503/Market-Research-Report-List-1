<p><p>Bonded carbide, also known as cemented carbide or tungsten carbide, is a composite material composed of hard carbide particles bonded together by a metallic binder. One of the major drivers for this growth is the increasing demand for cutting and drilling tools in industries such as automotive and aerospace. Bonded carbide is extensively used in the manufacturing of cutting tools, inserts, and wear parts, owing to its high hardness and resistance to wear and corrosion.</p><p>Additionally, the growing mining industry, particularly in developing countries, is fueling the demand for bonded carbide. It is widely used in the production of mining tools, such as drill bits and inserts, due to its ability to withstand high temperatures and abrasive conditions.</p><p>Furthermore, the advancements in material science and manufacturing processes have led to the development of new grades of bonded carbide with improved properties, such as higher toughness and heat resistance.
