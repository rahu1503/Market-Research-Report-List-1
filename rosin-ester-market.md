<p><p>Rosin ester is a type of chemical compound derived from rosin, a natural resin obtained from pine trees. The market for rosin esters can be divided into two main types: glycerol ester and pentaerythritol ester. Glycerol ester is produced by reacting rosin with glycerol, resulting in a product that offers excellent thermal stability and compatibility with various polymers. On the other hand, pentaerythritol ester is synthesized by reacting rosin with pentaerythritol, resulting in a product with superior heat resistance and adhesion properties. Both types of rosin esters find applications in industries such as adhesives, coatings, and printing inks.</p></p>
