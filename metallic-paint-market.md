<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The metallic paint market can be classified into two types: solvent-based and water-based paints. Solvent-based metallic paints are formulated with organic solvents, which evaporate during the drying process, leaving behind a durable and glossy finish. They have excellent coverage and adhesion properties, but they release volatile organic compounds (VOCs) that contribute to air pollution. In contrast, water-based metallic paints use water as a solvent, resulting in lower VOC emissions and easier cleanup. They offer good color retention, but may require more coats for an optimal metallic effect. Both types cater to different customer preferences and environmental regulations.</p></p>
