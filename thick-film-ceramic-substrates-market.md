<p><p>The thick film ceramic substrates market consists of two main types: single-layer thick film ceramic substrates and multilayer thick film ceramic substrates. Single-layer substrates are composed of a single ceramic layer, typically made from alumina or aluminum nitride, and are commonly used in applications requiring high thermal conductivity. Multilayer substrates, on the other hand, consist of multiple ceramic layers stacked together, offering enhanced functionality and design flexibility. These substrates are widely used in industries such as electronics and telecommunications for their ability to accommodate complex circuit designs.</p></p>
