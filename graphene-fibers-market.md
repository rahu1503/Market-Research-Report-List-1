<p><ul><li>Polymer Combined Fibers</li><li>Inorganic Metals Combined Fibers</li><li>Inorganic Nonmetals Combined Fibers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Graphene fibers are classified into three types: polymer combined fibers, inorganic metals combined fibers, and inorganic nonmetals combined fibers. Polymer combined fibers are formed by integrating graphene with polymers, enhancing their mechanical properties. Inorganic metals combined fibers are created by combining graphene with metallic materials, resulting in improved thermal and electrical conductivity. Inorganic nonmetals combined fibers are produced by incorporating graphene with non-metallic elements, elevating their strength and durability. These different types of graphene fibers offer unique properties and potential applications in various industries.</p></p>
