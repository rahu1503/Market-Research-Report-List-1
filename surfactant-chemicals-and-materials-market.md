<p><p>Surfactant chemicals and materials are widely used in various industries for their ability to lower the surface tension of liquids. They can be categorized into four main types: anionic, cationic, amphoteric, and nonionic. Anionic surfactants are negatively charged and commonly used in soaps and detergents. Cationic surfactants are positively charged and often found in fabric softeners and hair conditioners. Amphoteric surfactants have both positive and negative charges, making them suitable for a wide range of applications. Nonionic surfactants have no charge and are commonly used in creams and lotions for their gentle properties.</p></p>
