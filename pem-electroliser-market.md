<p><p>A PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane) electrolyser is a device that uses an electrolytic process to split water into hydrogen and oxygen gases. It consists of a solid polymer electrolyte membrane, which conducts protons, and two electrodes where the electrolysis occurs. PEM electrolysis is a popular technology due to its high efficiency, fast response time, and ability to operate at low temperatures.</p><p>The current outlook of the PEM electrolyser market is quite promising. The increasing demand for hydrogen as a clean fuel for transportation and various industrial applications is driving the market growth. Governments worldwide are focusing on reducing greenhouse gas emissions, which is leading to the adoption of renewable energy sources such as wind and solar power. PEM electrolysis systems are ideal for coupling with these sources to produce green hydrogen.</p><p>The market also benefits from the growing interest in energy storage solutions. Hydrogen obtained through PEM electrolysis can be stored and used later for power generation or fuel cell applications. Additionally, the use of hydrogen as a feedstock in various industrial processes further contributes to market growth.</p><p>In terms of future prospects, the PEM electrolyser market is expected to continue its upward trajectory. The increasing deployment of hydrogen infrastructure and the establishment of hydrogen refueling stations are further driving the demand for PEM electrolysis systems. Furthermore, ongoing research and development efforts to enhance the efficiency and reduce the cost of PEM electrolysis are expected to make the technology even more attractive in the coming years.</p><p>However, the market is not without its challenges. The high cost of PEM electrolysis systems compared to other electrolysis technologies is a hindrance to widespread adoption.
