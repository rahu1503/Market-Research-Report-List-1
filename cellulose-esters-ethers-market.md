<p><p>Cellulose esters and ethers are derivatives of cellulose, a naturally occurring organic compound found in plant cell walls. These compounds are extensively used in various industries such as food and beverages, pharmaceuticals, textiles, cosmetics, and paints and coatings, due to their excellent properties like biodegradability, low toxicity, and good film-forming ability.</p><p>Cellulose esters are synthesized by esterification of cellulose with carboxylic acids, while cellulose ethers are derived through the etherification of cellulose with alkyl or hydroxyalkyl groups. The most commonly used cellulose esters and ethers include methyl cellulose, ethyl cellulose, cellulose acetate, cellulose propionate, and cellulose butyrate.</p><p>The global cellulose esters and ethers market is forecasted to experience significant growth during the forecast period. Rising demand for eco-friendly and bio-based products is driving the market growth.
